# G14-05 — GRC Career Development — Roles, Skills, and Progression

> [!abstract] What This Note Covers
> By the end of this note, you will understand the GRC career landscape — the roles that exist, the skills each requires, the certifications that credential each level, progression paths from analyst to CISO, and how to position yourself effectively for GRC career advancement.

---

## The GRC Role Landscape

GRC spans a wide range of roles — from junior analysts implementing compliance requirements to CISOs governing enterprise security programmes. Understanding the landscape enables strategic career planning.

### Junior GRC Roles (0–3 years experience)

**Information Security Analyst (GRC focus)**
- *Responsibilities*: Maintaining policy documentation; supporting risk assessments; managing compliance evidence; supporting internal and external audits; responding to customer security questionnaires; tracking corrective actions.
- *Key skills*: Attention to detail; documentation skills; understanding of one or two compliance frameworks (ISO 27001; SOC 2; GDPR); Excel/Google Sheets; basic SQL for evidence queries.
- *Certifications valued*: CompTIA Security+; ISO 27001 Foundation; CISM (aspirational); GDPR Foundation.
- *Salary range (UK, London)*: £30K–45K.

**GRC Analyst**
- *Responsibilities*: Similar to IS Analyst but with more focus on risk — maintaining risk registers; conducting risk assessments; producing risk reports; supporting regulatory compliance management.
- *Key skills*: Risk assessment methodology; regulatory knowledge (GDPR; PCI DSS; sector-specific); report writing; stakeholder communication.
- *Certifications valued*: ISO 27001 Foundation or Lead Auditor; CISM; CRISC; GDPR Practitioner.
- *Salary range (UK, London)*: £35K–50K.

**Compliance Analyst**
- *Responsibilities*: Managing compliance with specific regulatory requirements (GDPR; PCI DSS; sector-specific); supporting audits; monitoring regulatory changes; managing compliance evidence.
- *Key skills*: Regulatory knowledge; compliance programme management; audit support; documentation.
- *Certifications valued*: GDPR Practitioner; ISO 27001 LA; PCI DSS QSA (advanced); sector-specific qualifications.
- *Salary range (UK, London)*: £32K–48K.

---

### Mid-Level GRC Roles (3–7 years experience)

**GRC Manager**
- *Responsibilities*: Managing the GRC programme (or a component of it) — risk management; compliance; policy management; audit programme. May manage junior GRC staff. Significant stakeholder engagement with business units and external parties (CBs; regulators; customers).
- *Key skills*: Programme management; stakeholder management; risk management; multiple framework expertise; leadership of junior staff.
- *Certifications valued*: CISM (near-essential at this level); ISO 27001 LA; CRISC; CISSP (for technical breadth).
- *Salary range (UK, London)*: £55K–80K.

**Information Security Manager**
- *Responsibilities*: Managing the organisation's information security programme — overseeing both GRC and security operations components. Reports to CISO or directly to senior management. Owns the ISMS; manages the security team.
- *Key skills*: Full ISMS lifecycle; team management; board communication; vendor management; incident response oversight.
- *Certifications valued*: CISM (essential); CISSP (strongly valued); ISO 27001 LA (valued for audit credibility).
- *Salary range (UK, London)*: £65K–90K.

**Privacy Manager / DPO**
- *Responsibilities*: Managing the organisation's privacy compliance programme; advising on GDPR obligations; managing DSAR responses; overseeing DPIAs; acting as contact for the ICO.
- *Key skills*: GDPR expertise; privacy impact assessment; cross-functional communication; regulatory relationship management.
- *Certifications valued*: CIPP/E (IAPP Certified Information Privacy Professional/Europe — the primary privacy qualification); OneTrust Privacy Professional; GDPR Practitioner/Auditor qualifications.
- *Salary range (UK, London)*: £55K–80K.

**Third-Party Risk Manager**
- *Responsibilities*: Managing the supplier security assessment programme; building and maintaining the supplier inventory; conducting due diligence assessments; managing the exception and monitoring processes.
- *Key skills*: Supplier assessment methodology; questionnaire management; contract review; risk management; stakeholder communication with procurement and legal.
- *Certifications valued*: CISM; CRISC; CTPRP (Certified Third Party Risk Professional — Shared Assessments).
- *Salary range (UK, London)*: £50K–75K.

**Internal Audit (IS/IT focus)**
- *Responsibilities*: Conducting audits of the information security programme; IT general controls; specific control areas. May be within the internal audit function or the security function.
- *Key skills*: ISO 27001 LA or CISA; audit methodology; evidence evaluation; finding writing; stakeholder management.
- *Certifications valued*: CISA (Certified Information Systems Auditor — ISACA; the primary IS audit qualification); ISO 27001 LA; CIA (Certified Internal Auditor — IIA).
- *Salary range (UK, London)*: £55K–80K.

---

### Senior GRC Roles (7+ years experience)

**Head of GRC / VP GRC**
- *Responsibilities*: Leading the entire GRC function — risk management; compliance; policy; audit; privacy; third-party risk. Reporting to CISO or directly to the board. Strategic GRC programme direction.
- *Key skills*: Full GRC programme leadership; board communication; enterprise risk management; regulatory engagement; senior stakeholder management; team leadership (5–15 staff).
- *Certifications valued*: CISM (essential); CISSP (strongly valued); ISO 27001 LA; CRISC.
- *Salary range (UK, London)*: £90K–130K.

**CISO / Chief Information Security Officer**
- *Responsibilities*: Full accountability for the organisation's information security programme — governance; risk; compliance; security operations; architecture; incident response. Board-level reporting. Strategic security leadership.
- *Key skills*: All GRC skills; security operations and architecture; board communication; P&L exposure; executive leadership; regulatory relationship management.
- *Certifications valued*: CISM (widely expected); CISSP (widely expected); board advisory experience; C-suite relationship skills that no certification can substitute for.
- *Salary range (UK, London)*: £120K–250K+ (highly variable by organisation size and sector).

## The GRC Certification Stack

**For a GRC professional targeting Head of GRC or CISO level, the optimal certification stack:**

*Year 1–2 (entry/mid level):*
- ISO 27001 Foundation (understanding; quick to achieve)
- ISO 27001 Lead Auditor (credibility for audit and implementation work)
- GDPR Practitioner (essential for any UK/EU GRC role)

*Year 3–5 (mid level):*
- CISM (the primary GRC management qualification)
- CRISC (risk management specialist; highly valued in financial services and consulting)

*Year 5+ (senior level):*
- CISSP (technical breadth; widely expected at CISO level)
- Domain-specific: CIPP/E (privacy); CTPRP (third-party risk); sector qualifications (GIAC for technical depth; CCSP for cloud)

**The GUARDIAN curriculum covers**: ISO 27001 LA; CISM; CRISC; CISSP Domain 1 and GRC-relevant domains. The curriculum is the knowledge foundation for this entire certification stack.

## Key GRC Skills Beyond Certifications

Certifications credential knowledge. These skills determine career progression:

**Written communication**: The GRC function produces an enormous volume of written content — risk reports; policy documents; audit findings; board presentations; regulatory correspondence; customer questionnaire responses. The ability to write clearly, concisely, and appropriately for different audiences is a core GRC differentiator.

**Stakeholder management**: GRC crosses every business function — IT; legal; finance; HR; operations; sales. The ability to build relationships with, influence, and communicate effectively with diverse stakeholders at all levels (from technical engineers to board directors) is the most important non-technical GRC skill.

**Regulatory intelligence**: The regulatory landscape changes constantly. GRC professionals who actively monitor regulatory developments — new guidance from the ICO; DORA updates; PCI DSS v5 previews; NIS2 implementation updates — provide more value than those who only know current requirements.

**Quantitative risk communication**: The ability to express risk in financial terms — ALE; expected loss; cost-benefit of controls — is increasingly expected at senior GRC levels. This is a skill that requires practice with real numbers, not just conceptual understanding.

**Project management**: Most GRC initiatives are projects — certification programmes; compliance remediation; risk assessment programmes; tool implementations. PM discipline (scope management; timeline tracking; stakeholder communication; resource management) is directly applicable and valued.

**Business acumen**: Understanding how the organisation makes money; what its strategic priorities are; what its regulatory obligations mean commercially — this contextual knowledge is what separates GRC professionals who govern and those who administrate.

## Career Pathways into GRC

### From Technical Security (Pentesting/Security Operations)

**Strengths**: Technical credibility; hands-on knowledge of controls; understanding of threats; incident experience.

**Gaps to close**: Governance and risk management mindset; regulatory and compliance knowledge; board communication skills; policy writing.

**Transition path**: 
1. Pursue ISO 27001 LA to build audit and governance framework knowledge
2. Move into a GRC Analyst or IS Manager role that values technical background
3. Add CISM to validate management capability
4. Build cross-functional stakeholder skills through deliberate exposure

**Your specific profile (Atharva)**: The penetration testing background + MSc Applied Cyber Security + CEH V12 provides exactly the technical credibility that strengthens GRC roles. The GUARDIAN curriculum and CISM study builds the governance layer. The combination is genuinely differentiating — most GRC professionals lack deep technical security knowledge; most security professionals lack GRC depth.

### From IT/Technology Management

**Strengths**: Technology understanding; IT operations knowledge; change management experience; ITIL familiarity.

**Gaps to close**: Risk management methodology; regulatory knowledge; security-specific control expertise.

**Transition path**: ISO 27001 Foundation → CISM → GRC Manager role.

### From Legal/Compliance (Non-Security)

**Strengths**: Regulatory expertise; documentation; stakeholder communication; risk-mindset.

**Gaps to close**: Technical security knowledge; ISMS implementation; control framework expertise.

**Transition path**: ISO 27001 Foundation → GDPR Practitioner → Privacy Manager → broader GRC roles.

## Positioning for GRC Roles: CV and Portfolio

**The GRC CV**: GRC roles are credential-heavy — certifications, frameworks, and regulatory expertise should be prominent. But the most powerful CV elements are specific programme outcomes:

*Weak*: "Responsible for ISO 27001 compliance."

*Strong*: "Led ISO 27001 certification programme for 250-person SaaS company — achieved certification within 14 months from a low-maturity baseline; managed Stage 1 and Stage 2 audits with 2 minor NCs; built the risk register from zero to 85 risks with documented treatment plans."

**Evidence of impact**: Quantify wherever possible — certifications achieved; risks managed; compliance gaps closed; audit findings resolved; customer security questionnaires managed.

**Framework coverage**: List all frameworks you have direct implementation or audit experience with: ISO 27001 (implementation/audit); ISO 27001 LA; GDPR/UK GDPR; PCI DSS; Cyber Essentials; SOC 2; NIST CSF. Depth of experience matters more than breadth — "ISO 27001 implementation lead for 3 years" outweighs "awareness of 8 frameworks."

**The GRC portfolio (for junior/mid-level roles)**: 
- Risk register examples (anonymised)
- Policy/standard examples (written by you)
- Audit report example (anonymised)
- Board security report example (anonymised)
- Evidence of certification support or management

**The GUARDIAN curriculum as portfolio content**: The 105 notes you have built are a demonstrable knowledge portfolio. The ability to discuss any of these topics in depth — risk assessment methodology; ISO 27001 Clause 9; GDPR Article 28 DPA requirements; audit finding classification — is visible evidence of preparation.

## GRC Interview Preparation

GRC interviews typically combine:

**Technical knowledge questions**: "Walk me through the ISO 27001 certification process." "What is the difference between a major and minor nonconformity?" "How would you conduct a risk assessment?" "What is a DPIA and when is it required?"

**Scenario-based questions**: "A major supplier notifies you of a breach. Walk me through your response." "The board wants to accept a risk that exceeds your risk appetite thresholds. What do you do?" "You've identified 200 risks in the first assessment. How do you prioritise?"

**Programme management questions**: "How have you built or improved a GRC programme?" "How do you get business unit buy-in for security requirements?" "What is your approach to communicating risk to non-technical executives?"

**Framework knowledge**: "Which frameworks have you worked with and at what depth?" "How does ISO 27001 relate to GDPR?" "When would you recommend SOC 2 over ISO 27001?"

**The CISM mindset in interviews**: Apply CISM thinking — governance before operations; risk-informed decisions; business alignment. Interviewers at GRC Manager level and above will assess whether you think like a security manager or like a security technician. The GUARDIAN curriculum gives you the knowledge; CISM thinking gives you the frame.

## The UK GRC Job Market

**High-demand sectors**: Financial services (FCA regulation; DORA for EU operations); healthcare (NHS DSP Toolkit; HIPAA for US-facing companies); technology/SaaS (ISO 27001 for enterprise customers; SOC 2 for US market); consulting (PwC; KPMG; Deloitte; EY all have significant GRC practices); critical infrastructure (NIS2 requirements driving GRC hiring).

**London premium**: London GRC salaries are typically 20–35% higher than regional UK roles. Remote GRC roles are increasingly available but the most senior roles still tend to be London-based.

**Consulting vs in-house**: Consulting (Big 4; specialist security consultancies) offers broad exposure to multiple frameworks and industries, faster skill development, and typically higher billing rates — but more demanding hours and travel. In-house roles offer deeper programme ownership, more stable hours, and the ability to see long-term programme impact.

**For your visa situation**: The GRC path into a SOC 2139 (Cyber Security Specialist) role under a skilled worker visa is very direct — GRC Manager and Head of GRC roles in financial services and technology regularly appear under this SOC code and command salaries well above the £38,700 general threshold.
